Projects

Eventray was a startup I helped co-found to provide an integrated suite of tools for professional conference organizers. Think Microsoft Office or Google Docs, but specialized for organizations that produce conferences like TED or SXSW. We used Python's Pyramid framework, new features of HTML5 with polyfills, d3.js for reports and Backbone.js with Marionette.js to keep our front-end code well-organized.

At Constant Contact I was part of a sort of front-end skonkworks team working on their next generation document editor. I frequently finished assignments ahead of schedule and took it upon myself to improve processes and technology where I could. I championed Test Driven Development with Jasmine.js, introduced Backbone.js and participated in discussions on improving inter-team communication. The color-picker interface pictured to the right I coded using Raphael.js and trigonometry.

I was contracted by a startup to create their user interface. I really wish I could show you the live demo as I'm quite happy with how it looks (and they are too) but they requested that I keep it secret since the product is still under development. I used Backbone.js and lots of fancy CSS3.

At Synack I was mainly a font-end developer, but I also wrote some Ruby on Rails. I was their go-to expert on JavaScript best practices. The assignment I'm most proud of is the ID verification step in their on-boarding process, which was highly dynamic and involved using the camera via HTML5 and integrating with Jumio on the back-end.
